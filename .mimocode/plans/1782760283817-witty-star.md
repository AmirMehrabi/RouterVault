# Homepage Rewrite — Basecamp-Style Landing Page

## Philosophy

Basecamp's landing page works because it:
1. **Leads with the problem**, not the product
2. **Uses opinionated, conversational copy** — "Meetings are toxic"
3. **Sells a philosophy**, not features
4. **Shows the old way vs the new way** — contrast creates desire
5. **Tells specific stories** — "It's 2am, a router breaks, nobody knows what changed"
6. **Pricing is simple and transparent** — no guessing
7. **Short sections, bold type** — easy to scan, hard to ignore

## Product Answers

- **Core pain**: Losing configs overnight — upgrade breaks, no rollback, hours of recovery
- **Opinionated stance**: "One router is fine. Fifty is chaos."
- **Tone**: Casual, confident, direct — engineer-to-engineer
- **Primary audience**: ISPs managing many MikroTik routers
- **Worst-case stories**: (1) Router upgrade with no backup to roll back to, (2) Tech changes something at 2am, nobody knows until customers call
- **Metrics**: Currently small (7 routers, 6 tenants) — use aspirational placeholders or skip
- **Pricing**: Free (EUR 0, 1 router), Starter (EUR 9/mo, 3 routers), Operator (EUR 19/mo, 10 routers), Extra (EUR 2/router/mo)

## New Section Structure

### 1. Hero
**Headline**: "One router is fine. Fifty is chaos."
**Subheadline**: "WISPA keeps every MikroTik router config backed up, versioned, and ready to roll back — so 2am emergencies don't become 2am disasters."
**CTAs**: "Start Free — No Credit Card" + "See How It Works"
**No hero image** — just bold type on white. Basecamp style.

### 2. The Problem (story-driven)
Tell two specific stories:
- **The upgrade disaster**: "You upgrade RouterOS at 10pm. Everything looks fine. At 6am, half your APs are offline. The config is gone. You have no previous version. Now you're rebuilding from memory."
- **The mystery change**: "A customer calls — their internet is down. You check the router. Someone changed the firewall rules last night. Nobody logged it. Nobody remembers."

Then the pivot: **"This isn't a technology problem. It's a systems problem."**

### 3. The Old Way vs The New Way
Side-by-side contrast:
| The Old Way | The New Way (WISPA) |
|---|---|
| Manual `/export` files scattered across desktops | Automatic backups on a schedule |
| "Who changed this?" with no answer | Every version tagged with who and when |
| Winbox open on one laptop | Web dashboard, access from anywhere |
| Hope nothing breaks | Roll back any version in seconds |

### 4. How It Works (3 steps, dead simple)
1. **Connect** — Add your MikroTik routers via API
2. **Back up** — WISPA pulls configs automatically on schedule
3. **Compare & roll back** — See what changed, revert when needed

### 5. What You Actually Get (outcomes, not features)
- **Sleep better** — Every config is backed up before you touch it
- **Blame less** — See exactly who changed what and when
- **Fix faster** — Compare any two versions side by side
- **Work anywhere** — Web-based, no Winbox required
- **Scale confidently** — Manage 10 or 1000 routers the same way

### 6. Pricing (simple, transparent, inline)
Four tiers in a clean grid:
- **Free** — EUR 0 — 1 router, 7-day history
- **Starter** — EUR 9/mo — 3 routers, 30-day history
- **Operator** — EUR 19/mo — 10 routers, 180-day history
- **Extra routers** — EUR 2/router/mo — Simple expansion

No feature comparison tables. Just what matters: router count and history depth.

### 7. FAQ (addressing objections)
- "Is my data secure?" — Yes, tenant-isolated, encrypted, API-only access
- "Does it work with RouterOS v6 and v7?" — Yes, both
- "What if I need more than 10 routers?" — Add extra routers at EUR 2/mo each
- "Can I self-host?" — Not yet, but it's on the roadmap

### 8. Final CTA
**Headline**: "Stop hoping nothing breaks."
**Subline**: "Start backing up your MikroTik configs for free. No credit card required."
**CTA**: "Create Free Account"
